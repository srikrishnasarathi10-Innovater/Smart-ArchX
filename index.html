Arduino Hardware Link Guide

Troubleshoot serial telemetry data feeds

01 USB-to-UART Bridge Driver Missing

If your Arduino board is plugged in but does not appear in the port list when clicking LINK DEVICE, your laptop is likely missing USB serial drivers. Install them and restart your browser:

download CH340 / CH341 Driver (Most Boards) download CP210x Driver (NodeMCU Boards)

02 Baud Rate Verification

If you connect successfully but see garbage text or nothing at all, make sure the baud rate in your Arduino sketch (e.g. Serial.begin(9600);) matches the Baud select dropdown in our header.

03 USB Cable Capabilities

Many standard micro-USB or USB-C cables are designed strictly for power charging and do not contain internal data lines. Swap your USB cable if the port list is completely empty or has no response.

04 Port Busy or Locks

Only one application can read a serial port at a time. Close the Arduino IDE Serial Monitor, serial plotters, or other browser tabs that might have locked the COM port.
